## 📊 Dashboard Overview

This project provides an in-depth exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the classic video game industry, visualizing how the market evolved from cartridge-based beginnings to the peak of the physical retail era and the transition into modern gaming.

### Key Visualizations & Sections:
1. **Sales Volume vs. Games Released by Year:** Tracks global revenue and publication volume over four decades, highlighting the 2008–2010 industry peak.
2. **Average Sales per Game:** Analyzes how market saturation impacted per-game commercial success (overall average: ~$0.54M).
3. **Regional Sales Mix Over Time:** Breaks down market share shifts between North America (NA), Europe (EU), Japan (JP), and Other regions.
4. **Top Platforms:** Ranks historic giants like the PlayStation 2 (PS2), Xbox 360, PS3, Wii, and Nintendo DS.
5. **Top Genres & Publishers:** Identifies commercial winners across categories like Sports, Shooters, and Role-Playing games, alongside publishing titans like Nintendo and Electronic Arts.
6. **Top Best-Selling Releases:** Spotlights legendary titles like *Wii Sports*, *Grand Theft Auto V*, *Super Mario Bros.*, and *Tetris*.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights & Findings

* **The 2008–2010 Peak:** Global sales and title releases reached an all-time high around 2008–2010, driven by the massive success of the Nintendo Wii, PS3, and Xbox 360 generation.
* **The Saturation Effect:** While the total volume of released games grew exponentially over time, the average sales per game dropped significantly, demonstrating that commercial success became heavily concentrated among blockbuster hits.
* **Regional Disparities:** 
  * **North America (NA):** Dominates the global market with **49.15%** of total sales.
  * **Europe (EU):** Represents a robust secondary market at **27.33%**.
  * **Japan (JP):** Accounts for **14.57%**, showcasing a unique preference for handheld systems, local RPGs, and Nintendo hardware.
* **Publisher & Platform Dominance:** **Nintendo** leads both the publisher rankings and individual hardware milestones (with the PS2 claiming the crown for standalone home console unit sales).
